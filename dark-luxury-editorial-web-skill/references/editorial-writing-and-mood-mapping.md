# Editorial Writing And Mood Mapping

Use this reference when you need stronger consistency in Chinese travel writing, tighter content-schema granularity, or a more reliable bridge from mood words to image search terms.

## 1. Content schema guidance

Use this as the default granularity unless the user requests otherwise.

### Hero

- title: as short as possible
- subtitle: one compact phrase or duration line
- supporting label: one uppercase travel mark
- cover line: 1 sentence only

### Day

- day title: 1 line
- day intro: 2 to 3 sentences
- quote: optional, 1 sentence

### Event / Beat

- title: 1 sentence fragment
- description: usually 1 to 3 sentences
- tags: 1 to 3 concrete tags only

### Auxiliary sections

- tips: 3 to 6 items
- packing: 4 to 8 items
- transport: short and decision-oriented
- budget: grouped by category with ranges

## 2. Chinese editorial microcopy rules

Aim for restrained magazine-style prose, not inflated travel brochure writing.

Do:

- anchor each paragraph with one concrete object, action, sound, smell, or light condition
- prefer tactile and sensory detail over generic praise
- let short sentences create breathing room
- compress adjectives
- keep rhythm calm and human

Prefer:

- “窗外的水气像一层旧玻璃”
- “走慢一点，把注意力放在墙面阴影和被雨打湿的地面”

Avoid:

- “这里真的非常治愈非常浪漫非常出片”
- “绝美、梦幻、神仙、氛围感拉满” style filler
- meta lines like “以下是为你整理的”

## 3. Good vs weak route-guide phrasing

Good:

- tells the user where, when, why, and what to watch for
- leaves room for atmosphere without sacrificing usefulness

Weak:

- piles up adjectives
- turns every stop into a slogan
- omits logistics and only keeps mood

## 4. Mood to visual mapping

Translate abstract mood into concrete image characteristics before sourcing.

### Calm / breathable

- light: dawn, blue hour, overcast daylight
- materials: water, linen, fog, soft foliage
- composition: negative space, mid-distance framing, quiet horizons
- search terms:
  - `editorial`
  - `calm`
  - `mist`
  - `breathable`
  - `quiet landscape`

### Humid / plant-rich

- light: after rain, diffuse cloud light, shaded window light
- materials: wet stone, leaves, moss, water reflections, condensation
- composition: layered greens, near-far depth, leaf foregrounds
- search terms:
  - `rainy`
  - `wet leaves`
  - `humid`
  - `green reflections`
  - `botanical`

### Old-magazine / editorial

- light: film-like low contrast, dusk, shadowed interiors
- materials: concrete, wood, paper, fabric, matte surfaces
- composition: restrained styling, asymmetry, generous margins
- search terms:
  - `editorial photography`
  - `cinematic`
  - `moody`
  - `film still`
  - `low saturation`

### Night-market / lively but controlled

- light: warm bulbs, wet pavement reflections, blue-black sky
- materials: steam, signage glow, food texture, crowds in partial blur
- composition: side-angle details, reflective surfaces, selective density
- search terms:
  - `night market editorial`
  - `wet pavement`
  - `food stall cinematic`
  - `blue hour city`

## 5. Search-term construction formula

When sourcing imagery, build queries like this:

`[destination or scene] + [time/weather] + [mood] + [editorial/cinematic]`

Examples:

- `Hangzhou West Lake blue hour editorial`
- `Xishuangbanna night market rainy cinematic`
- `Jingshan field window view editorial`
- `lakeside hotel misty morning cinematic`

## 6. When writing and shot list disagree

If a beautiful phrase suggests the wrong image:

- fix the shot list, not the facts
- keep the prose grounded in the actual route
- never source a semantically wrong image just because it matches the tone words
